Solar Powered Engineering

Harnessing the power of sunlight to drive innovation, efficiency, and sustainable transportation.

Project Overview

Our solar car project focuses on designing and building a small-scale vehicle powered entirely by solar energy. The car converts sunlight into electrical energy using photovoltaic panels, which then supply power to a DC motor to drive the vehicle forward. The goal is to explore renewable energy solutions while applying real-world engineering principles such as energy conversion, motor efficiency, material selection, and overall vehicle performance.

Through research, testing, and iteration, we are analyzing how solar panel placement, panel efficiency, motor specifications, and weight distribution affect the car’s speed and energy output. This project demonstrates how sustainable energy systems can be engineered to reduce reliance on fossil fuels and promote cleaner, more efficient transportation alternatives.

Engineering Design

We are testing different gear ratios, motor configurations, and panel placements to maximize speed and efficiency. Structural balance and weight distribution are also critical to maintaining optimal performance.

Testing & Performance

Performance is measured by analyzing speed, torque, and energy output under varying sunlight intensities to determine efficiency improvements.
