Windmill Engineering Project

Innovating Renewable Energy Through Design

Project Overview

Our windmill project focuses on designing and constructing a functional small-scale wind turbine capable of converting kinetic wind energy into electrical energy. The goal is to explore renewable energy solutions while applying real-world engineering principles such as aerodynamics, mechanical energy transfer, structural stability, and efficiency testing.

Through research, prototyping, and experimentation, we are analyzing how blade angle, blade shape, material selection, and wind speed affect power output. This project demonstrates how sustainable energy systems can be engineered to reduce reliance on fossil fuels and promote cleaner energy alternatives.

Engineering Design

We are testing different blade designs, materials, and structural layouts to maximize efficiency and durability. Each prototype undergoes wind testing to measure rotation speed, energy output, and stability under variable wind conditions.

By applying mechanical principles and material science, we aim to optimize the windmill for real-world energy production while maintaining safety and sustainability.
